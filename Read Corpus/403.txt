angilbert february sometimes known as saint angilbert or angilberk or engelbert was noble frankish poet who was educated under alcuin and served charlemagne as secretary diplomat and son in law he was venerated as pre congregation saint and is still honored on the day of his death february life angilbert seems to have been brought up at the court of charlemagne at the palace school in aquae grani aachen he was educated there as the pupil and then friend of the great english scholar alcuin when charlemagne sent his young son pepin to italy as king of the lombards angilbert went along as primicerius palatii high administrator of the satellite court as the friend and adviser of pepin he assisted for while in the government of italy angilbert delivered the document on iconoclasm from the frankish synod of frankfurt to pope adrian and was later sent on three important embassies to the pope in and at one time he served an officer of the maritime provinces he accompanied charlemagne to rome in and was one of the witnesses to his will in there are various traditions concerning angilbert relationship with bertha daughter of charlemagne one holds that they were married another that they were not they had however at least two sons and one daughter one of whom nithard became notable figure in the mid th century and the daughter bertha went on to marry helgaud ii count of ponthieu control of marriage and the meanings of legitimacy were hotly contested in the middle ages bertha and angilbert are an example of how resistance to the idea of sacramental marriage could coincide with holding church offices on the other hand some historians have speculated that charlemagne opposed formal marriages for his daughters out of concern for political rivalries from their potential husbands none of charlemagne daughters were married despite political offers of arranged marriages in angilbert retired to the abbey of centulum the monastery of st richarius at present day saint riquier in picardy elected abbot in he rebuilt the monastery and endowed it with library of volumes it was not uncommon for the merovingian carolingian or later kings to make laymen abbots of monasteries the layman would often use the income of the monastery as his own and leave the monks bare minimum for the necessary expenses of the foundation angilbert in contrast spent great deal rebuilding saint riquier when he completed it charlemagne spent easter of the year there in keeping with carolingian policies angilbert established school at saint riquier to educate the local boys
