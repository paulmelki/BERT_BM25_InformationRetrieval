alcidamas of elaea in aeolis was greek sophist and rhetorician who flourished in the th century bc life he was the pupil and successor of gorgias and taught at athens at the same time as isocrates to whom he was rival and opponent we possess two declamations under his name on sophists περὶ σοφιστῶν directed against isocrates and setting forth the superiority of extempore over written speeches more recently discovered fragment of another speech against isocrates is probably of later date odysseus perhaps spurious in which odysseus accuses palamedes of treachery during the siege of troy according to alcidamas the highest aim of the orator was the power of speaking extempore on every conceivable subject aristotle rhet iii criticizes his writings as characterized by pomposity of style and an extravagant use of poetical epithets and compounds and far fetched metaphors of other works only fragments and the titles have survived messeniakos advocating the freedom of the messenians and containing the sentiment that god has left all men free nature has made no man slave eulogy of death in consideration of the wide extent of human sufferings techne or instruction book in the art of rhetoric and phusikos logos lastly his mouseion word invoking the muses seems to have contained the narrative of the contest of homer and hesiod of which the version that has survived is the work of grammarian in the time of hadrian based on alcidamas this hypothesis of the contents of the mouseion originally suggested by nietzsche rheinisches museum appears to have been confirmed by three
