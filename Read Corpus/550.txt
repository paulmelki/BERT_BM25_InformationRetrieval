alypius of antioch was geographer and vicarius of roman britain probably in the late ad he replaced flavius martinus after that vicarius suicide his rule is recorded is ammianus xxiii life he came from antioch and served under constantius ii and was probably appointed to ensure that nobody with western associations was serving in britain during time of mistrust rebellion and suppression symbolised by the brutal acts of the imperial notary paulus catena he may have had to deal with the insurrection of the usurper named carausius ii alypius was afterwards commissioned to rebuild the temple in jerusalem as part of julian systematic attempt to reverse the of the roman empire by restoring pagan and in this case jewish practices among the letters of julian are two and addressed to alypius one inviting him to rome the other thanking him for geographical treatise which no longer exists references sources todd roman britain fontana london salway roman britain oxford
