dasyproctidae is family of large south american rodents comprising the agoutis and acouchis their fur is reddish or dark colour above with paler underside they are herbivorous often feeding on ripe fruit that falls from trees they live in burrows and like squirrels will bury some of their food for later use dasyproctids exist in central and south america which are the tropical parts of the new world the fossil record of this family can be traced back to the late oligocene deseadan in the salma classification as with all rodents members of this family have incisors pre molars and molars but no canines the cheek teeth are hypsodont and flat crowned classification fossil taxa follow mckenna and bell with modifications following kramarz family dasyproctidae genus alloiomys genus australoprocta genus branisamys genus incamys genus neoreomys genus megastus genus palmiramys genus dasyprocta azara agouti azarae coiban agouti coibae crested agouti cristata black agouti fuliginosa orinoco agouti guamara kalinowski agouti kalinowskii red rumped agouti leporina mexican agouti mexicana black rumped agouti prymnolopha central american agouti punctata ruatan island agouti ruatanica genus myoprocta green acouchi pratti red acouchi acouchy the pacas genus cuniculus are placed by some authorities in dasyproctidae but molecular studies have demonstrated they do not form monophyletic group references external links central american agouti at smithsonian dasyproctidae at animaldiversity org figueira et al carrion consumption by dasyprocta leporina rodentia dasyproctidae and review of meat use by agoutis
