the politics of antigua and barbuda takes place in framework of unitary parliamentary representative democratic monarchy wherein the sovereign of antigua and barbuda is the head of state appointing governor general to act as vice regal representative in the nation prime minister is appointed by the governor general as the head of government and of multi party system the prime minister advises the governor general on the appointment of council of ministers executive power is exercised by the government legislative power is vested in both the government and the two chambers of the parliament the bicameral parliament consists of the senate seventeen member body appointed by the governor general and the house of representatives seventeen seats members are elected by proportional representation to serve five year terms antigua and barbuda has long history of free elections three of which have resulted in peaceful changes of government since the general election the party system has been dominated by the antigua labour party alp for long time was dominated by the bird family particularly prime ministers vere and lester bird the opposition claimed to be disadvantaged by the alp longstanding monopoly on patronage and its control of the media especially in the general election the most recent elections to the house of representatives were held on june the antigua labour party government was elected with fourteen seats the united progressive party has three seats in the house of representatives constitutional safeguards include freedom of speech press worship movement and association antigua and barbuda is member of the eastern caribbean court system the judiciary is independent of the executive and the legislature jurisprudence is based on english common
