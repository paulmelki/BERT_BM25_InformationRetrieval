agapanthus africanus african lily is flowering plant from the genus agapanthus native to the area of cape of good hope in south africa africanus is more difficult to grow in gardens than praecox and almost all plants sold as africanus are actually praecox it has short stem bearing tuft of long narrow arching leaves cm long and cm broad and central flower stalk cm tall ending in an umbel of white or bright blue funnel shaped flowers each flower cm diameter cultivation it was introduced to europe at the close of the th century unlike the more common praecox it is not generally suitable as garden plant and will not tolerate extended freezing temperatures see also list of plants known as lily references external links plantweb agapanthus africanus
