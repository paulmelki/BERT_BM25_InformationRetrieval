alexander aetolus ἀléxandros aἰtōlós was greek poet and grammarian the only known representative of aetolian poetry life alexander was the son of satyrus and stratocleia and was native of pleuron in aetolia although he spent the greater part of his life at alexandria where he was reckoned one of the seven tragic poets who constituted the tragic pleiad alexander flourished about bc in the reign of ptolemy ii philadelphus he had an office in the library of alexandria and was commissioned by ptolemy to make collection of all the tragedies and satyric dramas that were extant he spent some time together with antagoras and aratus at the court of antigonus ii gonatas notwithstanding the distinction alexander enjoyed as tragic poet he appears to have had greater merit as writer of epic poems elegies epigrams and cynaedi among his epic poems we possess the titles and some fragments of three pieces the fisherman kirka or krika which however is designated by athenaeus as doubtful and helena of his elegies some beautiful fragments are still extant his cynaedi or ionic poems are mentioned by strabo and athenaeus some anapaestic verses in praise of euripides are preserved in gellius references sources further reading powell ed collectanea alexandrina reliquiae minores poetarum graecorum aetatis ptolemaicae enrico magnelli ed alexandri aetoli testimonia et fragmenta studi testi
