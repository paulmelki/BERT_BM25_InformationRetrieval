fragment of greek work by anthemius on paradoxes of mechanics fragment un ouvrage grec anthèmius sur des paradoxes de mècanique anthemius of tralles medieval greek anthémios trallianós was greek from tralles who worked as geometer and architect in constantinople the capital of the byzantine empire with isidore of miletus he designed the hagia sophia for justinian life anthemius was one of the five sons of stephanus of tralles physician his brothers were dioscorus alexander olympius and metrodorus dioscorus followed his father profession in tralles alexander did so in rome and became one of the most celebrated medical men of his time olympius became noted lawyer and metrodorus worked as grammarian in constantinople anthemius was said to have annoyed his neighbor zeno in two ways first by engineering miniature earthquake by sending steam through leather tubes he had fixed among the joists and flooring of zeno parlor while he was entertaining friends and second by simulating thunder and lightning and flashing intolerable light into zeno eyes from slightly hollowed mirror in addition to his familiarity with steam some dubious authorities credited anthemius with knowledge of gunpowder or other explosive compound mathematics anthemius was capable mathematician in the course of his treatise on burning mirrors intended to facilitate the construction of surfaces to reflect light to single point he described the string construction of the ellipse and assumed property of ellipses not found in apollonius of perga conics the equality of the angles subtended at focus by two tangents drawn from point his work also includes the first practical use of the directrix having given the focus and double ordinate he used the focus and directrix to obtain any number of points on parabola this work was later known to arab mathematicians such as alhazen eutocius commentary on apollonius conics was dedicated to anthemius exterior of the hagia sophia architecture as an architect anthemius is best known for his work designing the hagia sophia he was commissioned with isidore of miletus by justinian shortly after the earlier church on the site burned down in but died early on in the
