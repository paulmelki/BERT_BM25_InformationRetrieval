andronicus of rhodes was greek philosopher from rhodes who was also the scholarch head of the peripatetic school he is most famous for publishing new edition of the works of aristotle that forms the basis of the texts that survive today life little is known about andronicus life he is reported to have been the eleventh scholarch of the peripatetic school he taught in rome about bc and was the teacher of boethus of sidon with whom strabo studied works of aristotle andronicus is of special interest in the history of philosophy from the statement of plutarch that he published new edition of the works of aristotle and theophrastus which formerly belonged to the library of apellicon and were brought to rome by sulla with the rest of apellicon library in tyrannion commenced this task but apparently did not do much towards it the arrangement which andronicus made of aristotle writings seems to be the one which forms the basis of our present editions and we are probably indebted to him for the preservation of large number of aristotle works writings andronicus wrote work upon aristotle the fifth book of which contained complete list of the philosopher writings and he also wrote commentaries upon the physics ethics and categories none of these works is extant two treatises are sometimes erroneously attributed to him one on emotions the other commentary on aristotle ethics really by constantine paleocappa in the th century or by john callistus of thessalonica notes references further reading external links the rediscovery of the corpus aristotelicum with an annotated bibliography attribution
