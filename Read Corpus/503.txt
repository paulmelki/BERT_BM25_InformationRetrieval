alexander of epirus bc bc also known as alexander molossus was king of epirus bc of the aeacid dynasty as the son of neoptolemus and brother of olympias alexander was an uncle of alexander the great he was also an uncle of pyrrhus of epirus life neoptolemus ruled jointly with his brother arybbas when neoptolemus died in bc his son alexander was only child and arrybas became the sole king in bc alexander was brought to the court of philip ii of macedon in order to protect him in in his late philip made him king of epirus after dethroning his uncle arybbas when olympias was repudiated by her husband in bc she went to her brother and endeavoured to induce him to make war on philip alexander however declined the contest and formed second alliance with philip by agreeing to marry the daughter of philip alexander niece cleopatra during the wedding in bc philip was assassinated by pausanias of orestis in bc alexander at the request of the greek colony of taras in magna graecia crossed over into italy to aid them in battle against several italic tribes including the lucanians and bruttii after victory over the samnites and lucanians near paestum in bc he made treaty with the romans he then took heraclea from the lucanians and terina and sipontum from the bruttii through the treachery of some lucanian exiles he was compelled to engage under unfavourable circumstances in the battle of pandosia and was killed by lucanian he left son neoptolemus and daughter cadmea in famous passage livy speculates on what would have been the outcome of military showdown between alexander the great and the roman republic he reports that as alexander of epirus lay mortally wounded on the battlefield at pandosia he compared his fortunes to those of his famous nephew and said that the latter waged war against women references external links lendering jona alexander of molossis livius org birth and kingship dates are incorrect
